04 How we measure success

Two metrics. One success, one guardrail.

Success metric
ARPU
Holds steady or grows vs counterfactual.
What it tells us. If ARPU holds, it means we captured the price increase without losing the demand that justified it. The price aligned to market, the customer kept buying. That's the win.
Guardrail
Gross Margin
Does not drop below baseline.
What it tells us. If GM drops sustainedly on any treated destination, the price is eroding margin instead of capturing it. That triggers a rollback. We don't expect this to happen, but it's the line we won't cross.
05 What happens after D+14

Three possible outcomes. Each one has a clear next step.

Scale
ARPU holds. GM stable.

The price absorbed well. Apply the rate to the next currency batch and consider a second step toward 1.17.

Iterate
Mixed signals.

ARPU holds but GM compresses near tolerance. Hold the rate, investigate, review before advancing further.

Rollback
ARPU drops. Or GM breaks.

The price did not absorb. Revert to 1.08 across all treated destinations. Lesson logged.

06 What we learned from EXP-001

Last year's USD attempt taught us two things.

May 2025

A +10% move with limited controls

The actual gap at the time was 6.55%; the experiment moved +10%. With today's lens, that move was larger than the gap justified. AOV was the primary read and held steady, but the broader effect on customer behavior wasn't centered in the verdict.

May 2026

A measured move, with the right metric

+6.48% on a real 8.33% gap. Smaller, deliberately conservative. ARPU as the success metric, capturing price, conversion and mix together. A clean control. If something doesn't work, we know within 14 days.
