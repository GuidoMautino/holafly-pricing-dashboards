00 Hypothesis
What we set out to falsify

Correcting the £3.79 PPD should lift conversion and short-plan revenue.

If

we apply the standard €3.79/day PPD logic to the 63 key GBP destinations — resulting in -21% on days 1-3, converting £3.79 / £7.58 / £11.37 to £2.99 / £5.99 / £8.99,

→
Then

conversion in the short-plan segment will improve and short-plan revenue will increase,

→
Because

correcting the first price-per-day impression reduces the price barrier on the cheapest plans, and short plans have minimal data cost, so volume gains more than compensate the lower price per order.

D+14 Verdict
Hypothesis partially confirmed. Conversion improved (+1.26pp full funnel). Short-plan volume grew significantly (+33.7% in the clean window). Elasticity confirmed at the channel level (App ε=-2.60). But ARPU vs counterfactual missed the +8% threshold by 0.2pp, so the experiment did not pass the success metric as declared.

Confounders identified · how we mitigated each

Six confounders affected this experiment. Each is mitigated by a specific methodological choice. Reading the report without these mitigations risks attributing to the price change movements that came from elsewhere.

01 Calendar
Easter (April 5, 2026)
Where
Inside the counterfactual projection window (Apr 1-14), not in the post-launch observation window (Apr 8-22).
Mitigation
Counterfactual €4.85 declared in brief. Reported as-is per LEARN-160. Effect absorbed into the gap.
02 Platform
App pricing issue from Apr 15
What
App orders registered at old price levels in BigQuery from Apr 15. Web unaffected. Confirmed in Argentina USD: platform-level.
Mitigation
Primary read = Web-only for 14 days. Channel-level elasticity only on 6-day clean window. App excluded from Apr 15 onwards.
03 Channel
App vs Web asymmetry
What
Different elasticities: App ε=-2.60, Web ε=-0.39 (clean window). Structural pattern, confirmed cross-market in MXN.
Mitigation
Elasticity reported by channel separately. Aggregate -1.60 shown as context, not as actionable read.
04 Tooling
Adobe scope dilution
What
Treated scope = short plans, ~3.4% of GBP revenue. Adobe can only measure full GBP funnel = 100%.
Mitigation
ARPU verdict reported as-is with explicit dilution caveat. Open question to Xabi on appropriate metric for future PPD.
05 Demand
GBP-wide demand surge in April
What
Reference buckets 4-7d: +23.4%. Reference buckets 8-14d: +29.5%. Untouched, no price change. General demand growth.
Mitigation
DID with internal control absorbs this. Control group exposed to the same surge. The +€10K-€83K range is surge-adjusted.
06 Data scope
COGS scope alignment
What
Of the 63 treated destinations, 57 recorded data in both pre and post windows. Six had no data in one window.
Mitigation
Same 57 destinations applied symmetrically to pre and post. Blended GM% diff vs initial 63: 0.16pp (immaterial).
