did not pass.

We applied a ~10% FX correction across all 207 JPY destinations and a PPD correction that lowered 1-day prices -30% in 65 destinations. Despite higher prices on average (+9.7%), ARPU declined -1.5% because the mix shifted toward cheaper short plans.

Verdict
The experiment did not pass the success metric (ARPU). Conversion improved +2.6pp and gross margin improved +7.1pp, but ARPU declined and the decline is statistically confirmed (p=0.039).

More visitors converted (+2.6pp). But they bought a cheaper mix of plans on average. The conversion uplift was not enough to offset the price reduction in 65 destinations and the resulting mix shift toward short plans.

At D+14 we recommended SCALE and framed the ARPU decline as "expected by design." That was a framing error. If ARPU is the success metric and it's negative, the experiment didn't pass.
02 Why ARPU dropped

Two opposite price forces under one blended metric.

The experiment applied two simultaneous changes. The FX correction raised prices ~10% across all 207 destinations and all durations. The PPD correction lowered 1-day prices -30% in 65 destinations (and 2-day -7% in the same 65). Everything else went up.

In the 65 destinations where 1-day prices dropped -30%, short-plan volume grew +19% while the comparable group (non-PPD, +10% prices) was flat. But this volume came at roughly break-even revenue (+2.7%). The price reduction drove orders without generating incremental revenue.

Share of short-plan orders shifted from 49.1% (pre) to 53.5% (sustainability) toward the PPD 65 destinations. This mix shift toward cheaper plans is the primary contributor to the blended ARPU decline.

Design error: two opposite forces under one blended metric made a clean pass impossible. Learning: one change, one hypothesis, one evaluation.
03 Action

Revert the PPD correction. Keep the FX.

Revert
PPD correction in 65 destinations
Return 1-2 day plans to pre-experiment prices in the 65 destinations where prices went down. This is the testable hypothesis that can be isolated and re-evaluated on its own, with ARPU as success metric.

Timeline: revert within 2 weeks. 30 days stabilization. Then re-test PPD isolated.
Maintain
FX correction across all destinations
The FX correction updated the internal rate from 159.31 to 175 JPY/EUR because we were charging ~10% below market. Today the gap has already grown back to ~7% (175 vs 187 JPY/EUR). Reverting would widen an operational margin loss.

This is not an experiment hypothesis. It's an alignment to market conditions.

The PPD correction drove +19% more orders in 65 destinations, but revenue was roughly break-even (+2.7%). The price reduction generated volume without incremental revenue. The non-PPD group (prices up +10%) was stable in volume and grew revenue +4.0%.

Long plans 4+d: both groups declined similarly

PPD 65 destinations: -31.9% volume. Non-PPD 137: -25.6%. Consistent with seasonal decline confirmed by Singapore benchmark (SGD long plans also declined ~26%) and YoY data. Not a differential pricing effect. GM improved +7 to +12pp across all long-duration buckets.
